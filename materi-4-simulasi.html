The apparent contradiction between high estimates of the a priori probability of extraterrestrial civilizations and the lack of evidence for, or contact with, such civilizations is known as the Fermi Paradox. However, many researchers propose the existence of a "Great Filter": an evolutionary barrier that is statistically extremely difficult for life to overcome.

This filter could exist at any stage of evolution. If the filter is in our past, it implies that complex life is extraordinarily rare, and humans are likely alone. Conversely, if the filter lies in our future, it suggests that advanced civilizations almost inevitably destroy themselves before mastering interstellar travel.

Therefore, discovering fossilized bacteria on Mars would be devastating news. It would indicate that the emergence of life is common (not the filter), implying the Great Filter lies ahead of us, potentially dooming humanity to extinction.
